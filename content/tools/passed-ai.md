Passed.ai addresses a specific and growing problem: AI-generated content that has been deliberately modified to evade detection. As AI humanizer tools proliferated through 2023 and 2024, students and content producers began using them to rewrite AI output before submitting it, exploiting the blind spots in first-generation detectors.

The tool's detection model was trained with this evasion pattern in mind. It analyses text for structural and statistical signatures that persist even after paraphrasing — patterns in sentence construction, word choice distributions, and semantic coherence that differ between genuinely human writing and humanized AI output.

The sentence-level view is particularly informative here. Because humanized AI content is often inconsistent — some sentences emerge from the rewrite looking more natural than others — the per-sentence breakdown helps reviewers identify the passages most likely to be AI-origin even within a document that passes a global-level check.

LMS integration covers Canvas, Blackboard, and Moodle. The PDF report output is designed for institutional documentation — the kind of evidence trail that academic integrity proceedings require. Institutional pricing is available for schools and universities that want to deploy it at scale.

As a paid-only tool without a meaningful free tier, Passed.ai is best suited to institutions or professional educators who have a specific problem with humanized AI submissions, rather than individual users doing occasional checks.
