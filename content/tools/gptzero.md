GPTZero was built by Edward Tian in January 2023 as a direct response to the release of ChatGPT. It became one of the defining tools of the AI detection category and remains the reference point for education-focused use cases.

The core detection model analyses text for perplexity (how unpredictable the word choices are) and burstiness (the variation in sentence complexity) — two metrics that tend to differ between human and AI writing. The interface shows an overall AI probability score, a sentence-by-sentence breakdown, and, on paid plans, a writing process timeline that shows how a document was composed.

For educators, the dashboard view is the standout feature. Instructors can upload an entire class set of submissions and review results in bulk, flag borderline cases for manual review, and generate reports. Integrations with Canvas and Google Classroom allow submissions to flow directly into GPTZero without file exports.

The free tier covers basic single-document detection with usage limits. The Educator plan unlocks batch uploads, the writing process viewer, and LMS integrations. Pricing is institutional — universities typically license it at the department or faculty level.

One important caveat: GPTZero, like all detectors, produces false positives. Non-native English speakers and writers with formal academic styles are occasionally flagged. GPTZero has published guidance on this limitation and recommends treating results as one signal among several rather than definitive proof.
