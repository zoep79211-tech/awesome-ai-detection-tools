Copyleaks has been in the plagiarism detection business since 2015 and added AI content detection in 2023. The combination of a long operational history, extensive language support, and deep LMS integrations gives it a strong position in international educational markets where English is not the primary language.

The 100+ language capability is the headline differentiator. Most AI detectors are trained predominantly on English text and perform poorly on other languages. Copyleaks claims meaningful accuracy across major European, Asian, and Middle Eastern languages, though performance varies by language and model.

LMS integrations cover Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, and several others through a Turnitin-compatible plugin interface. For institutions that have already integrated Copyleaks for plagiarism checking, adding AI detection requires no additional setup.

The API is comprehensive: endpoints cover text analysis, file upload (PDF, DOCX, HTML), batch processing, and webhook callbacks for asynchronous large-volume jobs. An enterprise SDK is available for teams that want to embed detection directly into their own products.

White-labelling is available for EdTech companies and institutional technology teams that want to offer AI detection under their own branding. This makes Copyleaks one of the few detection tools that can be repackaged as a component of a larger product.
