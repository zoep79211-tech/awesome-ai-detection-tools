Content at Scale built its reputation as an AI content generation platform before adding a free detector as a companion tool. The detector reflects the platform's content-marketing orientation: it prioritises the metrics that matter to editors and SEO managers rather than academic integrity officers.

The paragraph-level breakdown is the most useful feature. Rather than a single document-level score, the tool assigns a probability to each paragraph, making it easy to identify which sections of a long article need revision. This is more actionable than a global percentage when the task is editing rather than auditing.

The human content score is a composite metric that combines AI probability with readability and quality signals, giving a rough indication of how well the content will perform from both a search engine and reader engagement perspective. This framing — detection as part of a broader content quality workflow — distinguishes it from tools that treat detection as a binary pass/fail.

No account is required for the free detector, and there are no hard word limits on single scans, though very long documents may be processed in sections. The free tool is a loss-leader for the broader Content at Scale platform, which is a paid AI content generation service.
