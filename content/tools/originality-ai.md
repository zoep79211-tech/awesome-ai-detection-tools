Originality.ai was one of the first tools to combine AI detection with plagiarism checking under a single credit system — a practical approach for content teams that need both capabilities without managing two separate subscriptions.

The tool operates on a credit model: each credit covers 100 words of analysis. Credits are bought in bundles and never expire, which suits agencies with variable monthly volumes. A team dashboard lets managers assign credits to individual users and review scan history across the organisation.

The bulk URL scanning feature is a practical differentiator. Rather than pasting article text manually, teams can feed a list of published URLs and have Originality.ai fetch and scan them automatically — useful for auditing a content library or vetting a site acquisition.

The Chrome extension integrates detection into the browsing experience. Editors reviewing draft content in Google Docs or a CMS can trigger a scan without leaving their workflow.

Accuracy on standard AI-generated content is strong. The tool is more conservative than some competitors, meaning it tends to produce fewer false positives at the cost of occasionally missing lightly edited AI content. For agencies where incorrectly flagging a human writer's work would be damaging, this trade-off is usually preferable.

The API is well-structured and popular with developers who want to automate content quality checks as part of a publishing pipeline.
