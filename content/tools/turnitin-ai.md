Turnitin's AI detection capability launched in April 2023, adding a dedicated AI writing indicator to the originality reports that millions of educators already use. The integration is seamless for institutions already on the platform — no new tool to learn, no separate login, no change to the submission workflow.

The detection model flags text as potentially AI-generated at the paragraph level, alongside the existing similarity percentage. Instructors see a combined view: how much of the document resembles published sources (plagiarism) and how much reads as AI-generated (AI writing). The two scores are independent — a document can score high on one, both, or neither.

Turnitin's AI detection accuracy in independent testing has been competitive with dedicated detectors, though the company publishes its own internal benchmarks rather than third-party audits. The false positive rate has been a documented concern: in 2023 several high-profile cases emerged of students disputing AI flags, leading Turnitin to add a manual review workflow and lower its confidence thresholds.

Because Turnitin is enterprise-licensed, it is not accessible to individual educators or students outside an institutional subscription. The tool's value is inseparable from the broader Turnitin ecosystem — it is most useful when an institution already relies on Turnitin for originality checking and wants AI detection without adding a separate vendor.
