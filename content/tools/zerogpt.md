ZeroGPT is one of the most widely used free AI detectors, primarily because it removes all friction from the detection process. There is no account creation, no email verification, and no credit system to manage — paste text and get a result in seconds.

The output is a straightforward percentage score indicating the likelihood that the text is AI-generated, accompanied by a sentence-level breakdown that highlights suspicious passages. The interface is intentionally simple: a text box, a button, and a result. This makes it accessible to users who are not technically sophisticated and just want a quick answer.

The browser extension extends this simplicity to the browsing context, letting users scan web page text or content in other browser-based tools without switching tabs.

Accuracy is generally rated lower than premium alternatives, and the tool has a reputation for more variable false positive rates than paid options. For casual use — a student checking their own writing, a blogger spot-checking an article — this level of accuracy is usually sufficient. For high-stakes institutional decisions, a more rigorously validated tool is preferable.

The free tier covers a generous amount of text per day. Paid plans remove limits and add features like batch processing and API access.
