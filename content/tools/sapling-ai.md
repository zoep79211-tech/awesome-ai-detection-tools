Sapling occupies an unusual niche in the AI detection landscape: it is aimed at business operations rather than academic integrity. The primary use case is monitoring and flagging AI-generated content in customer-facing communications — support tickets, sales emails, live chat — where authenticity and brand consistency matter.

The CRM integrations are the core differentiator. Sapling embeds directly into the agent interface of Salesforce Service Cloud, Zendesk, HubSpot, Intercom, and several other platforms. Agents see detection indicators inline, without switching tools, which makes it practical for high-volume support environments where checking each response separately would be impractical.

The real-time detection mode analyses text as it is being written or pasted, providing immediate feedback. This is different from the document-upload model of most detectors and better suited to the asynchronous, conversation-by-conversation nature of support workflows.

Sapling also includes grammar and autocomplete features, positioning it as a broader writing assistant rather than a dedicated detector. This means the detection capability is bundled with other tools that support teams are already motivated to use, lowering the adoption barrier compared to a standalone detection product.

The API allows custom integrations for organisations with non-standard tech stacks. The free tier covers individual use with rate limits; team and enterprise plans unlock higher volumes, admin controls, and dedicated support.
